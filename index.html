New York City
the city that never sleeps
🗽 USA ♡ my home base last visited: always
Haidilao 海底捞
Times Square
中餐 · hotpot
The service is almost aggressively attentive — they'll make you noodles tableside while you wait. The spicy broth is worth the two-hour queue. Bring a group.
🕐 open late💸💸💸
Carbone
Greenwich Village
意大利菜
Red-sauce Italian done with theatrical precision. The spicy rigatoni is everything. Book months in advance or try your luck at the bar — worth it every time.
🕐 dinner only💸💸💸💸
Sushi Yasuda
Midtown East
日式美食
Omakase counter where the fish does all the talking. No soy sauce needed — they season everything perfectly. One of those places that resets your standards.
🕐 lunch & dinner💸💸💸💸
Blank Street Coffee
Lower East Side
café
Tiny, efficient, and somehow always perfectly lit for your notes. The oat matcha latte is genuinely good. When you just need to disappear for two hours.
🕐 7am – 7pm💸
Butler
Midtown East
café
Australian-style café with proper flat whites. The avocado toast is a cliché that actually delivers here. Bright windows, warm wood — feels like a different city.
🕐 7am – 5pm💸💸
Broadway — TKTS Booth
Times Square
theater
Day-of discounted Broadway tickets. Show up an hour before the matinée and you'll almost always find something. That's how I first saw Hamilton for $60.
🕐 day-of only💸💸
The Public Theater
Astor Place
theater
Off-Broadway with soul. Shakespeare in the Park tickets are free (though the line starts at 6am). The building itself feels like theater — old and expectant.
🕐 evenings💸 – 💸💸
Central Park — The Ramble
Upper West Side
park
Not the main mall — go deep into The Ramble. Migratory birds stop here in spring. You'll forget Manhattan exists for exactly 40 minutes. Bring a thermos.
🕐 dawn is best💸 free
The High Line
Chelsea / Meatpacking
park
An elevated rail track turned garden — sounds gimmicky, is genuinely lovely in early morning before the tourists arrive. The wildflower sections in May are perfect.
🕐 7am – 10pm💸 free
City Reliquary
Williamsburg, Brooklyn
weird spot ✨
A tiny museum of random NYC artifacts — old subway tokens, Statue of Liberty fragments, Coney Island sand. $7 and 45 minutes will leave you oddly emotional about this city.
🕐 weekends only💸
Forgotten New York Walking
Various locations
weird spot ✨
Not a place — a practice. Follow forgottennewyork.com routes to find ghost signs, sealed subway stations, and lampposts from 1908. The city has layers most people never see.
🕐 any time💸 free
